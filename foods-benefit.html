Foods Most Likely to Benefit From Testing

Heavy Metals

Lead, Arsenic, Cadmium, Mercury

  • Dark chocolate and cocoa products
  • Spices (cinnamon, turmeric, chili powder)
  • Rice and rice-based foods
  • Seafood (especially large predatory fish)
  • Seaweed and seaweed snacks
  • Leafy greens
  • Root vegetables
  • Legumes

PFAS

Forever Chemicals

  • Seafood (fish and shellfish) — Identified as the most common PFAS-contaminated food category
  • Red meat — Linked to higher PFAS levels in a large population study
  • Eggs — Also associated with elevated PFAS in the same study
  • White rice — Strongly associated with higher PFAS levels in consumers
  • Coffee — Consumption correlated with increased PFAS levels
  • Ultra-processed foods — Found to contain PFAS in UNC Gillings research, including ready-to-eat meals
  • Dairy products — Can contain PFAS from contaminated soil, water, or feed
  • Vegetables and produce — PFAS can enter via contaminated soil or irrigation water
  • Grains — PFAS detected in grains grown in contaminated areas
  • Beverages (including coffee, juices, bottled drinks) — PFAS can enter through contaminated water sources

Microplastics

Plastic Particle Contamination

  • Seafood (fish and shellfish)
  • Sea salt
  • Bottled water
  • Tea brewed from plastic tea bags
  • Breaded seafood and processed seafood products
  • Plant-based meat alternatives
  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Beverages (including juices and bottled drinks)
  • Coffee

Pesticides

Agricultural Chemical Residues

  • Strawberries
  • Spinach
  • Grapes
  • Peaches
  • Pears
  • Nectarines
  • Apples
  • Bell peppers
  • Hot peppers
  • Cherries
  • Blueberries
  • Blackberries
